History

Persian traditional medicine is one of the most ancient forms of traditional medicine. The practice and study of medicine in Persia has a long and prolific history. The Iranian academic centers like Gundeshapur University (3rd century AD) were a breeding ground for the union among great scientists from different civilizations. These centers successfully followed their predecessors’ theories and greatly extended their scientific research through history. Persians were the first establishers of the modern hospital system.

Persian traditional medicine is grounded in the concept of four humors: phlegm (Balgham), blood (Dam), yellow bile (Ṣafrā') and black bile (Saudā'). The four humors concept is based on the teachings of Rhazes and Avicenna into an elaborate medical system. Different ratios of these groups of material in the body form the four essential temperaments which explain the excess or lack of warmness and humidity in our bodies. In Persian culture, everything has a temperament; this includes your organs, the seasons, colors, and even food!

For example, if you are a mixture of Phlegmatic and Sanguine Temperaments (hot damp/moist), you will need to be choosing foods that are cooling and drying such as beef, pork, and sour fruits. A Choleric Temperament on the other hand will be affected adversely by hot and dry foods such as chicken, and chickpeas, eggs, and least affected by cold and damp foods. During summertime, a Choleric Temperament needs to be eating cooling foods, as they will become quickly imbalanced if they are eating hot foods. Therefore, it is important to understand your individual Temperament!

Characteristics of the Four Temperaments

People with choleric aka "warm and dry" temperament:
  • are usually tall and thin. They have a small frame, their fingers, lips, and other body parts are not large, and they are unlikely to get overweight.
  • have a dry and warm skin color with a yellow and sometimes peachy undertone.
  • have a rapid pulse, are exuberant, quickly finish what they are supposed to do usually perfectly and carefully and speak fast.
  • digest food quickly, and as they do not have adequate amount of nutrients to provide for the energy needs of the body, they get hungry quickly.
People with phlegmatic aka "cold and wet" temperament:
  • usually feel cold and run colder than other people.
  • are light, fair and smoothed-complexioned, and relatively speaking they have glowing skin. They have silky, straight, smooth and thin hair. They do not have much unwanted hair.
  • are normally bulky and quickly gain weight with a body composition of larger proportions of fat rather than muscle tissue, and they usually seem swollen.
People with sanguine aka "warm and wet" temperament:
  • are normally sociable and cheerful. They are usually in high spirits, patient, and adaptable.
  • have a rosy complexion and soft and smooth skin. They also have thick black hair, with thick density and more volume.
  • are stout, sturdy, and burly with a muscular body.
  • have bigger veins which are prominent and easily seen through the skin in the arms and legs. They also have a more powerful and stronger pulse.
People with melancholic aka "cold and dry" temperament:
  • are usually thin and have a small frame. They do not have large body parts and they do not gain weight.
  • have a dark complexion and are likely to develop dark spots on their skin.
  • have hair that is coarse, curly and coiled. They also have excessive unwanted hair.
  • tend to be introspective and introverted. They act and speak unhurriedly, and might need more time to accomplish a task, but they do it well.
